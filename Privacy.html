Privacy Policy

HeliPlan

Last updated: June 27, 2026

The short version: This app does not collect, store, or share any personal information. We operate no servers and never receive your data. What you enter stays on your device - with one exception if you choose: your aircraft, bucket, and equipment libraries sync across your own devices through your private iCloud account, which is handled entirely by Apple and never reaches us. The only information that leaves your device for a lookup is a location you ask about (or your current location, if you tap the location button), which is sent to public weather and mapping services purely to look up elevation, temperature, weather, and place names. We never receive, store, or sell that information, and it is not tied to your identity.

This Privacy Policy explains how the HeliPlan app (the "App") handles information. The App is provided by HeliPlan Team ("we," "us"). By using the App, you agree to the practices described here.

Information we collect

We do not collect any personal information. We do not operate any servers, we do not have user accounts, and we do not use analytics, advertising, or tracking technologies of any kind.

Information you enter into the App - such as aircraft details, mission inputs, settings, your pilot signature, and any API key you choose to add - is stored on your device. Your aircraft, bucket, and equipment libraries are additionally synced through your own private iCloud account so they stay consistent across the Apple devices you sign in with (see Data storage and retention below). In every case we have no access to any of it.

Location information

The App can use a location in two ways: a place, airport, or coordinates you type in, or your device's current location if you tap the current-location button. Location is used to look up local elevation, temperature, weather, and flight conditions, and to calculate distances for your route.

To perform those lookups, the relevant coordinates (or the city and state derived from them) are sent to the third-party services listed below. This happens only when you request a location-based lookup. We do not store your location, and it is never linked to your identity.

Your device will ask for permission before the App accesses your current location. You can grant or deny this at any time in your device's Settings, and the App works with manually entered locations regardless.

Third-party services

When you request a location-based lookup, the App contacts the following services. Each has its own privacy practices, which we do not control:

ServiceWhat is sentPurpose
Apple Geocoding Coordinates Convert coordinates to a city/state and time zone
National Weather Service (weather.gov) Coordinates Forecast and current conditions
Aviation Weather Center (aviationweather.gov) An area around the coordinates, for METAR/TAF lookups; nothing location-specific for winds aloft METAR and TAF reports, flight category, and winds-aloft forecasts
Open-Meteo (open-meteo.com) Coordinates Ground elevation
U.S. GSA (gsa.gov) City and state Per-diem lodging and meal rates

We encourage you to review these providers' privacy policies. We share only what is necessary to perform the requested lookup and nothing that identifies you.

Winds-aloft forecasts are retrieved by downloading a single nationwide dataset and selecting the relevant data on your device. Your specific location is not sent to the Aviation Weather Center for that lookup.

Data storage and retention

Most of your data lives only on your device. The exception is your aircraft, bucket, and equipment libraries, which the App syncs through Apple's iCloud key-value storage so they stay consistent across the Apple devices signed in to your iCloud account.

That sync uses your own private iCloud storage and is handled entirely by Apple under your Apple ID. We operate no servers, we receive none of this data, and we cannot see it. Apple's handling of iCloud data is governed by Apple's Privacy Policy. You can turn the sync off at any time by disabling iCloud for the App in your device settings, or by signing out of iCloud.

Deleting the App from your device permanently removes the data stored locally on that device. Any libraries already synced to iCloud remain in your iCloud account and can be removed through your device's iCloud settings.

Children's privacy

The App is not directed to children and does not knowingly collect information from anyone, including children.

Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the "Last updated" date above. Continued use of the App after changes take effect constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
